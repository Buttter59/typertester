The Basics

What is WPM?

Words Per Minute (WPM) is the standard measure of typing speed. In most typing tests a "word" is defined as five characters, including spaces. This makes it fair to compare speeds regardless of word length.

Formula: (Characters typed ÷ 5) ÷ Minutes elapsed

Raw WPM counts every word attempted including errors. Net WPM (what TyperTester shows) subtracts errors — rewarding accuracy alongside speed. A 100 WPM typist at 95% accuracy is more productive than a 120 WPM typist at 75% accuracy.

Consistency measures how stable your speed was throughout the test. High consistency (80%+) means your speed was steady. Low consistency means you had significant bursts and slowdowns.

Formula
(chars ÷ 5) ÷ minutes
Example: 300 characters in 1 minute = 60 WPM
One "word" = 5 chars
"hello" = 1 word
Spaces count as characters
Accuracy matters as much as speed

Benefits of typing faster

Career Productivity
Going from 40 to 80 WPM cuts your typing time in half — freeing hours every week for deeper, higher-value work.
🧠
Think Without Friction
When typing is slow, your fingers can't keep up with your thoughts. Faster typing means ideas flow directly to the screen.
✉️
Faster Communication
Professionals who type faster respond faster and communicate more clearly, reducing bottlenecks in team workflows.
👨‍💻
Coding Speed

How to Improve

10 proven ways to type faster

Tip 01
Learn Touch Typing First
Place fingers on home row (ASDF–JKL;) and never look at the keyboard. Use the Hand Guide above to learn which finger covers each key. This single change unlocks dramatically higher speeds.
Tip 02
Accuracy Before Speed
Speed follows accuracy. If you're making frequent errors, slow down until you can type at 98%+ accuracy, then gradually increase pace. Rushing creates bad habits that are hard to unlearn.
Tip 03
Practice Daily
15–30 minutes of focused daily practice beats a 3-hour weekend session. Muscle memory builds through repetition over time, not cramming.
Tip 04
Use All Ten Fingers
Each finger has assigned keys. Left index covers F and G; right index covers J and H. Using all ten fingers distributes the work evenly and enables true speed.
Tip 05
Type Real Text
Practicing with actual sentences and stories trains rhythm, punctuation habits, and the word patterns that appear in real writing. TyperTester uses real passages for this reason.
Tip 06
Read Ahead While Typing
Fast typists look ahead — reading the next word while their fingers handle the current one. This eliminates the micro-pause between words that slows most typists down.
Tip 07
Fix Your Posture
Sit upright, keyboard at elbow height, monitor at eye level. Wrists should float above the keyboard. A good setup removes physical friction that drags speed down.
Tip 08
Drill Your Weak Keys
Most typists have 3–5 keys they consistently mistype. Check your heatmap on the Progress page to find which letters slow you down. Drill those specific combinations.
Tip 09
Increase Difficulty Gradually
Start with Easy passages until you hit 50+ WPM, then move to Normal, then Hard. Level up when you can maintain 95% accuracy comfortably.
Tip 10
Track Your Progress
Seeing your WPM trend upward is one of the most motivating things in typing practice. Use the Progress page to watch your scores climb over days and weeks.
